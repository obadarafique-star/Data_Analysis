Mobile Apps Playstore Data Analysis
Project Description

This project analyzes a comprehensive dataset of mobile applications published on the Playstore, examining key trends in app ratings, genres, categories, versions, and install counts. The analysis provides actionable insights into the mobile app marketplace, exploring user preferences, market competition, and factors influencing app popularity and success.

Dataset Overview

    Source: Internal data extract of Playstore apps.

    Size: Over 10,800 app records across multiple genres and categories.
    
Columns: App Name, Category, Rating, Reviews, Size, Installs, Type (Free/Paid), Price, Content Rating, Genres, Month, Last Updated, Current Version, Android Version.
Additional sheets: App categories, app genres, app versions, and star ratings are included for deeper exploration.

Business Requirements

   . Identify the most popular app categories and genres by install count.
   . Investigate relationships between ratings, installs, and app types (Free vs Paid).
   . Analyze compatibility trends across Android versions.
   . Provide recommendations based on market and user preference patterns.
    
Data Preparation

   . Duplicate and irrelevant entries were cleaned prior to analysis.
   . Genre, category, and install figures were aggregated for trend identification.
    
Key Findings:

Genre and Category Breakdown

   . Casual, Arcade, Action, and Family genres dominate the market, with Arcade leading installs (over 10.7 billion), reflecting broad appeal for easy-to-play games.
   . Tools, Communication, Productivity, and Entertainment are strong non-game categories with high install figures.
   . The dataset spans over 30 unique categories and 120+ genres, highlighting market diversity.

Ratings and Reviews

   . Consistently high ratings (4.0+) are found among top apps with more installs, though review count varies, showing that installs don’t always translate to more user reviews.   
   . Detailed analysis of star ratings can identify what drives user satisfaction.

Android Version Compatibility

   . The most common minimum compatibility is Android 4.1 and up (2,451 apps), followed by 4.0.3 and up (1,501 apps).
   . Developers often target older Android versions for broader device reach.    ​

App Type and Pricing

   . Over 90% of apps in the dataset are Free, with Paid apps a minority, generally in niche categories or with advanced features.
   . Typical paid app price ranges from INR 10-400.
   
Visualizations

   . Ratings distribution and install count plots

Recommendations

   . Future work could include sentiment analysis on reviews, install trend forecasting, and deep-dive genre-specific user analysis.
   . Consider merging external market and user data for richer insights.
